UHAVE & BAIWANZHUANG

About Baiwanzhaung

Located in the west district of Beijing, Million town is the first ever designed and built community socialhousing in the People’s republic of China in 1953. It once had the highest qualitied apartment units tailored to house intellectuals, with similar education level and family sizes selected by the government from different parts of the country, to come and resident in the capital city for work convenience.
This project served as a prototype of communal living that many neighborhoods residence followed to develop, creating residential blocks that had close neighborhood bonds. Nevertheless, as time passes, Million town is currently a near obsolete residential block, that the government considered to demolish and reform but decided to keep due to its historical value. Most residents are elders, renters from other cities and families with children attending the local primary school. The main issue is that the community lacks order and management. Waste, used furniture and building materials are dumped in public areas, some ground level residents even excavated the land for gardening. With no regulation privatizing the limited public space created safety hazards and disturbs the living of other residents.
Where is the future of Baiwanzhuang community?

A Sustainable Baiwanzhaung with UHAVE

To bring a better future, UHAVE has joined to bring equity and liveliness back to this neighborhood with the help of technology and the internet.
UHAVE will facilitate Million town to once again become the innovative model of an environmentally friendly and technology embraced open community.

How UHAVE Helps

  1. drones will be sent out to detect occupied public zones
  2. Scan abandoned objects/waste in the public space
  3. Upload image and information about scanned objects

During the beginning stage of the community transition to the IDEAL plan, drones will be sent out by UHAVE to first detect waste and occupied public zones, then scan the objects and finally upload information about these unwanted objects. (Clothes, furniture, garbage……) Residents will have the option to either give up these uploaded properties for the community to recycle and reuse or remove these objects from the public space and exchange it with other residents that has messaged.
